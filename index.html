THE PROBLEM

Most New Shopify Stores
Die Inside Ninety Days

It is almost never effort. It is almost always the wrong three decisions made before a single product goes live.

KILLER 01

Niche Picked On Passion

Owners pick what they love instead of what the data says will sell. Cat supplies. Yoga leggings. Phone cases. Saturated, crowded, almost impossible to rank.

KILLER 02

No Real Product Research

Products get listed without a supplier sample, without margin checks, and without a look at competitor store depth. One-star review avalanche follows.

KILLER 03

No Traffic Plan

The store launches. Then nothing. No ads, no SEO, no content strategy. The owner waits for customers who will never find them.

Do I need any Shopify experience to start?

No. Most members join with zero Shopify experience. The first module is a full walk-through of the set-up, so the first day is not a mystery.

Is this dropshipping, print on demand, or both?

Both. Separate training tracks for each, plus guidance on which route suits your budget and niche. You do not have to pick on day one.

Can I cancel any time?

Yes. Month to month, no long contract. Cancel inside your account in a couple of clicks. Lose access the day the billing period ends.

How much money do I need to start a store?

Realistic starter budget is $500 to $1,500 for Shopify fees, a design or two, samples, and a small ad test. The Clubhouse lays out exactly where each pound or dollar should go.

Who is this NOT for?

People looking for overnight results. A real Shopify store needs 90 to 180 days of consistent work before the numbers clear up. If you need cash this month, this isn't the route.
